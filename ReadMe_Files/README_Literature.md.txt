Dataset Overview
The literature dataset provides extensive coverage of literary studies across multiple domains:

Core Literary Areas:

Literary Devices (~32 problems) - Metaphor, symbolism, irony, personification, alliteration, imagery

Poetry Analysis (~245 problems) - Forms, meter, rhyme schemes, techniques, famous works

Narrative Elements (~327 problems) - Character, plot, setting, theme, point of view, conflict

Genres (~48 problems) - Novel, drama, poetry, gothic, mystery, fantasy, etc.

Authors & Works (~29 problems) - Shakespeare, Dickens, Austen, Morrison, García Márquez

Literary Movements (~32 problems) - Romanticism, Modernism, Beat Generation, Harlem Renaissance

Critical Analysis (~300 problems) - Interpretation, criticism approaches, textual analysis

Creative Writing (~289 problems) - Dialogue, voice, character development, storytelling techniques

Additional Coverage:

World Literature - Comparative studies, cultural contexts, translation

Reading Strategies - Active reading, annotation, close reading, textual evidence

Literary Theory - Feminist criticism, psychoanalytic theory, postcolonial studies

Drama & Theater - Dramatic structure, tragedy, comedy, theatrical techniques

Mythology & Folklore - Archetypal patterns, hero's journey, cultural narratives

Key Features:

500 exactly formatted rows with instruction, input, output columns

Varied instruction types: "What" questions (351), explanations (46), "How" instructions (45)

Multiple difficulty levels from basic definitions to advanced literary analysis

Comprehensive explanations with examples and context

Educational focus suitable for literature students and educators

Content Quality:

Factually accurate information about authors, works, and literary concepts

Clear, educational explanations with specific examples

Balanced coverage across different literary periods and traditions

Practical application of literary analysis skills

Integration of theoretical knowledge with practical reading and writing skills

The dataset uses empty input fields since most literary questions are self-contained in the instruction. Each output provides detailed, educational responses that would effectively train language models to understand and explain literary concepts, analyze texts, and guide readers in developing critical thinking skills about literature.
